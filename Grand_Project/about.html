First mentioned in 1392 in documents as a street leading to the Beaver Gate. In German, its name sounded like de beverportenstrate, as in N.-it. de bever - "beaver". These gates were located at the end of today's Audēju street, near Kaleju (Forge) street. In the 15th-16th centuries, the street’s name, Bobrovaya (beverstrate), took root. Somewhat later, a modified form of weverstrate, later Weberstraße. Weaving street(“Ткацкая”) was official name during the rule of Russian Empire. In the XIX century in Latvian, the street was called Vever (Latvian. Vēvēru iela). After independence, the street was named Audēju (replacing Germanism vēveris with a synonym for Latvian origin).

Initially, this street reached only Skarnu street. At the beginning of the XIX century was connected with the Grecinieku street.
