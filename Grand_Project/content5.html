The building is located in the historical centre of the city – in Old Riga that is part of the UNESCO World Heritage List.

According to historical sources the oldest data about the estate in Audēju Street 9 date back to 1458; then, there was a mansion that belonged to a man named Hans Hofburn. The history of the building preserved until today reaches back for more than 200 years. Within the centuries of its existance the property has had several owners. In the 19th century it belonged to a member of the Riga City Council, later it was rented out to the Treasury and for a short while it was even used as a stable. From the beginning of the 20th century the building has been mostly in commercial use and has housed different shops, chemical-bacteriological laboratory, a pastry shop, a canteen and several workshops. For years it has served as a Project Institute of the Communal Ministry.

In 2008 the building was acquired by new owners – the whole bouling was thoroughly renovated and is now one of the most beautiful and sumptuous examples of Classical and Art Nouveau architecture in Audēju Street. The exquisite, tasteful and modern interior with its luxurious and detailed decoration creates a presentable impression.
